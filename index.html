Our Friend Leo

2012
Physical
Digital
Interaction
Website

Leo is a guitar with a personality and a voice to express it. The Duncan/Channon Makers gave him sensors, a microcontroller, and a Twitter account. With these, he can live-tweet his guitar experiences any time something happens, like when someone plays him.

Our Friend Leo was inspired by the strong relationships people have with their possessions; we already name our cars, our iPods, and yes, our guitars. What if our possessions felt the same way about us? How would they express their feelings? With that in mind, we designed and created a device to give an inanimate object a digital presence, to incorporate the physical object into our virtual social world.
—
Visit ourfriendleo.com.
Collaborators: Hannah Puckett, Timothy Trieu, and Robin Yang.

Mookies

2011
Physical
Digital

Tangible objects that record and play back audio samples. A user can be creative in exploring the sonic properties of everyday objects, using each Mookie as a container for their uniquely recorded sounds. Multiple Mookies can be used to create an orchestra of sounds.
